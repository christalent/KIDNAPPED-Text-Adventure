# KIDNAPPED! — Game Analysis & Maps
# Source: RetroGamesTroove review + clean BASIC source + original play

## GAME OVERVIEW
- **Author:** Peter Kirsch, 1980
- **Published:** SoftSide Magazine (type-in adventure)
- **Platform:** TRS-80 / S-80 (16K machines)
- **Rooms:** 65 locations across 9 floors
- **Premise:** Kidnapped, escape floor by floor

## FLOOR THEMES & PUZZLES

### Floor 9 (Roof) — STARTING FLOOR
**Theme:** Elevator repair, midnight blackout puzzle

**Key Puzzle (from review):**
> "The exposed wires providing power to the elevator are 'hot' — you're going to need to coordinate your repairs with a building blackout that occurs at midnight."

**Items:** Chair, flashlight, electrical tape
**Mechanic:** TIME variable. You must wait until after midnight to safely tape the live wires.

**Room Count:** ~20 rooms
**Difficulty:** Moderate — the midnight timing adds complexity

### Floor 8 (Aquarium)
**Theme:** Piranhas, guard dog, helium balloon

**Key Puzzle:**
> "The next level is a relatively short one which involves an aquarium filled with piranhas, a guard dog, and some rope."

**Items:** Super glue, helium tank, balloon, rope, gun

**Room Count:** ~9 rooms (21-29)
**Difficulty:** Moderate

### Floor 7 (Bank Vault)
**Theme:** Burglar, dollar, vault

**Key Puzzle:**
> "The seventh level... might be the most difficult level. It involves trying to wrest a dollar from a burglar."

**Items:** String vending machine, tank of helium, gun, balloon, wood glue, wooden step

**Room Count:** ~9 rooms (31-39) + vault rooms (55-57)
**Difficulty:** HARDEST floor according to review

### Floor 6 (Office)
**Theme:** Sexy young office girls, knitting

**Key Puzzle:**
> "Level six involves some sexy young office girls and having to fashion yourself a new suit. Knitting needles as well as a ball of yarn come into play here."

**Items:** Knitting needles, ball of yarn

**Room Count:** ~9 rooms (41-49)
**Difficulty:** Moderate

### Floor 5 (Mary Poppins)
**Theme:** Absurdist, Mary Poppins umbrella

**Key Puzzle:**
> "There is no greater example of this than finding Mary Poppin's Umbrella on level five."

**Items:** Umbrella, knitting, sponge
**Difficulty:** Absurdist

### Floor 4 (Jekyll/Hyde)
**Theme:** Transformation, diving board

**Key Puzzle:**
> "On the next floor you quite literally have to experiment and turn yourself into Mr. Hyde in order to deal with the steel door."

**Items:** Potion, umbrella
**Mechanic:** DRINK potion → transform into Hyde
**Difficulty:** Moderate

### Floor 3 (Greenhouse)
**Theme:** Plants, trap door, flute

**Key Puzzle:**
> "The next level involved a small plant, a trap door, a water cooler, paper cup, flute, and rope."

**Items:** Plant, flute, water cooler, cup
**Mechanic:** Plant transformation, music
**Difficulty:** Moderate

### Floor 2 (Quicksand)
**Theme:** Quicksand, piano, book

**Key Puzzle:**
> "The last level involves a small book, some quicksand, rope, and a piano. It was another challenging level."

**Items:** Book, rope, piano
**Mechanic:** PLAY piano to stretch rope across quicksand
**Difficulty:** HARD (3rd hardest)

### Floor 1 (Ground) — ESCAPE!
**Theme:** Front entrance, alligator, burglar's mask

**Key Puzzle:**
> "A burglar's mask blocks your way east. He has the remains of the kidnapper in his mouth."

**Items:** Alligator, stairs
**Mechanic:** Navigate past final obstacles to escape
**WIN CONDITION:** Reach front door

## DIFFICULTY RANKING (from review)

## KEY INSIGHT: Midnight Blackout
The Floor 9 elevator puzzle requires coordinating with a building blackout at midnight:
- Wires are LIVE until after 11 PM
- Must WAIT (TIME passes) before safely taping wires
- This is the key detail the OCR missed

## PLAYER MEMORY
Chris played this in the early 80s on his TRS-80 — the midnight blackout mechanic is authentic to the original game design.
